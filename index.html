How to Use the Wheel Spinner, Dice, Coin Flip & Lucky Dip

The Wheel Name Picker is a free, easy-to-use random spinner designed for Australian teachers, classrooms, families, games and anyone who needs a fun and fair way to make a decision. Alongside the wheel you'll find a free coin flip, dice roller and lotto number generator — all on this one page.

Step 1 — Choose Your Mode

Select a preset mode from the coloured buttons at the top. Choose from Names, Numbers, Colours, Yes/No, Chores, What's for Dinner, Venue Picker, Day Picker, Games, or Custom. Each mode loads a ready-made list so you can start spinning immediately. Need to pick a random weekday for an activity or competition? The Day Picker has Monday through Sunday ready to go.

Step 2 — Customise Your List (Text or Pictures)

Use the panel on the right to add, remove or shuffle items. Type a name or item into the input box and press Add or hit Enter. Want a picture wheel instead? Tap the 📷 button to attach a photo to your next item — great for picking between meals, toys, activities or even photos of your kids or students. Your list updates the wheel instantly and automatically adjusts the segments.

Step 3 — Spin, Flip, Roll or Dip

Click the big SPIN button and watch the wheel go, with a sound effect and the wheel slowing to a stop on a randomly selected item. Or scroll down to flip a coin for heads or tails, roll one or two dice, or generate a free set of lucky dip lotto numbers. Every result is shown clearly with sound effects you can mute any time.

Step 4 — Remove Picked Names

After a name is picked, click Remove from wheel to take it off so it won't be picked again. This is perfect for classroom roll calls, competition draws and games — work through the whole list without repeating anyone. Use Reset All to bring everyone back for the next round.

Tips & Ideas for Using a Spinner Wheel

A random spinner wheel is one of the most versatile classroom and family tools available. Here are creative ways Australian teachers, families and organisers are using the Wheel Name Picker:

Classroom & Homework Uses

  • Cold call questions — spin to pick which student answers next, keeping everyone engaged and attentive
  • Reading groups — randomly assign students to groups for pair or group work
  • Class jobs & chores — spin each week to assign classroom responsibilities fairly
  • Homework or play — let the wheel decide what comes first, or pick a random homework topic to start with
  • Topic selection — let students spin to choose their research topic or project theme
  • Brain breaks — add activities like "10 star jumps", "sing a song", "tell a joke" for fun transitions
  • Reward draws — add student names who earned a ticket and spin for weekly prizes

Games, Competitions & Lotto

  • Prize draws & raffles — the fairest way to run a competition or giveaway
  • Lucky dip lotto numbers — generate a free random number set for any lottery format
  • Team selection — randomly divide players into teams for sports, trivia or games
  • Turn order — spin the wheel or flip the coin to decide who goes first in board games or card games
  • Dice-based games — use the dice roller for board games, drinking games (18+) or maths practice

Family & Home Uses

  • Chore wheel — spin to fairly assign weekly chores, tasks or jobs to each family member
  • Dinner decider — end the "what's for dinner" debate with a spin
  • Picture wheel for kids — attach photos of toys, snacks or activities so younger children can pick visually
  • Coin toss for small decisions — heads or tails for quick, simple choices
  • Weekend plans — add activity options and spin to choose your adventure

Why Random Is Better Than Choosing

Research in education shows that random selection in classrooms increases student engagement and attentiveness. When students know they could be picked at any moment, they stay focused and prepared. It also removes any perception of favouritism — the wheel, dice and coin all treat everyone equally.

For competitions and games, a random spinner, dice roll or coin flip ensures fairness that everyone can see and trust.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Wheel Name Picker completely free? ▼

Yes — completely free. No sign up, no account, no downloads required. The site is supported by advertising so the tool can remain free for everyone.

Is the result truly random? ▼

Yes. The wheel, coin, dice and lucky dip generator all use JavaScript's built-in random number generator. Every item, side or number has an equal chance of being selected, regardless of position or how many times other items have been picked before.

Can I use it for competitions and giveaways? ▼

Absolutely. The Wheel Name Picker is perfect for competitions, prize draws, social media giveaways and raffles. Add all your entrant names, spin live, and everyone can see the result. The pick history panel records each winner so you have a record of the draw.

Can I add pictures to the wheel instead of names? ▼

Yes. Tap the 📷 button next to the add-item box to attach a photo to your next entry. This works great for younger kids choosing between pictures of toys, meals or activities.

Can I generate random lotto numbers? ▼

Yes — the Lucky Dip tool generates random number sets for any lottery format. Use a Quick Pick preset for standard Australian lotto formats, or set your own count and number range.

Can I save my custom lists? ▼

Your custom list is saved in your browser's local storage while you're on the page. If you close the browser your list will be cleared.

Can I turn off the sound effects? ▼

Yes — use the Sound Effects toggle in the stats panel to turn the spin, dice and coin sounds on or off. Your preference is remembered while you're on the page.

Does it work on phones and tablets? ▼

Yes — fully responsive and works on all devices including iPhones, Android phones, iPads and tablets.
