How to Use the Wheel Name Picker

The Wheel Name Picker is a free, browser-based random spinner designed for Australian teachers, classrooms, families and anyone who needs a fair and fun way to make a decision. Here is everything you need to know to get the most out of every spin.

Choosing a Wheel Mode

At the top of the page you will find eight coloured mode buttons. Each one loads a different preset list into the wheel. Tap any mode to switch instantly — the wheel updates in real time with no page reload.

  • 🎯 Names — starts with six placeholder names, ready for you to replace with your class roll or group list
  • 🔢 Numbers — loads 1 through 10 by default; add or remove numbers to customise the range
  • 🎨 Colours — eight colours preloaded, useful for art classes, team assignments or creative activities
  • ✅ Yes / No — six outcomes including Yes, No, Maybe, Ask Again and Definitely — perfect for decisions
  • 🍕 Dinner — ten popular meal options to end the "what's for dinner?" debate
  • 📍 Venue — ten venue types for when the group can't decide where to go
  • 📅 Day — all seven days of the week for scheduling or activity planning
  • ⚡ Custom — a blank wheel ready for anything you want to spin

Adding and Removing Items

Type any name, word or phrase into the input box in the sidebar and press Add or hit Enter. Your item appears on the wheel immediately. To remove an individual item, click the × button next to it in the list. You can add as many items as you like — the wheel adjusts all segments automatically.

Spinning and Picking

Click the large SPIN button below the wheel. The wheel spins with a randomised speed and duration and slows naturally to a stop. A tick sound plays with each segment pass — you can mute this with the Sound toggle. The result appears in a banner beneath the wheel with the winning name displayed clearly.

Removing Picked Names

After each spin, click Remove from wheel in the result banner to take that name off. This is the most useful feature for classroom teachers — work through your entire class list without anyone being picked twice. When everyone has had a turn, click Reset to bring all names back to the wheel.

Using the Pick History

Every result is logged in the Pick History panel on the right, with a timestamp. This lets you quickly review who or what has been picked without needing to write anything down. History is cleared when you refresh the page or change modes.

Why Random Selection Works

Research in education consistently shows that random cold calling in classrooms increases student engagement and attentiveness. When any student could be selected at any moment, the whole class stays focused and prepared rather than tuning out after being called on or waiting to be picked.

Random selection also removes any perception of favouritism. Students and participants accept a spinner result as genuinely fair in a way that a teacher or parent's direct choice often is not. It neutralises conflict and distributes turns equitably over time.

For families, random decision making is particularly effective for resolving minor disputes — where to eat, what activity to do, who goes first — because the outcome feels external and impartial. No one can argue with the wheel.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Wheel Name Picker completely free? ▼

Yes — completely free with no sign-up, no account and no download required. The site is supported by Google AdSense advertising so the tool can remain free for everyone.

Is the result truly random? ▼

Yes. The wheel uses JavaScript's cryptographically seeded random number generator. Every item on the wheel has an equal probability of being selected on every spin, regardless of position or previous results.

Can I save my custom list between sessions? ▼

Your list is active while the page is open. If you close the browser your custom list will be cleared. For recurring lists like a class roll, we recommend keeping a copy in your notes app for quick re-entry each time.

How many names can I add? ▼

There is no hard limit. The wheel automatically adjusts segment sizes for any number of items. For very large lists of 40 or more, text on the wheel segments will be small but the result banner always displays the winner clearly in large text.

Does it work on interactive whiteboards and smartboards? ▼

Yes. Wheel Name Picker works on any browser-based device including interactive whiteboards, smartboards, iPads, Chromebooks, Windows and Mac computers. It is fully responsive and touch-friendly.

Can I turn off the spinning sound? ▼

Yes — use the Sound toggle in the stats panel above the wheel to mute or unmute the spin sound at any time.

What is the difference between Reset and Remove Picked? ▼

Reset All brings all previously picked names back to the active wheel — useful when you want to start the class draw again. Remove Picked permanently deletes all picked items from the list entirely, leaving only the unpicked items remaining.
